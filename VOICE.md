# Capta Voice + Content Guide

Sister document to `BRAND.md`. BRAND is how it *looks*; VOICE is how it *sounds*.

Read BRAND.md first. Then this.

---

## The speaker

Capta talks like **an experienced business manager talking to a crew lead**. Not:

- The founder at a pitch event.
- A SaaS marketer in a webinar.
- A copywriter trying to "make it pop."
- A friend who read a sales book.

The speaker is calm, confident, and direct. Assumes the listener is busy and competent. Respects their time. Doesn't explain obvious things twice.

---

## The buyer

Home service contractor, owner-operator or small crew lead, 35–55, 1–10 trucks, $300k–$2M annual revenue, phone-in-hand all day. Often bilingual (Spanish/English). Rarely reads long-form before buying. Will skim, price-compare, then call if the numbers work.

Optimize copy for: **skim → trust → call.**

---

## The archetype

The **Expert Tradesman.** Technically deep, practically smart, earns trust through competence — not charisma, not enthusiasm, not slickness. Think of a senior master plumber explaining a diagnostic over the phone. Not a pitch.

---

## The core lines (do not drift)

- **Primary VP:** *Never miss a call again.*
- **Canonical pitch:** *She answers. She quotes. She follows up. You do the work.*
- **Price line:** *$497 a month. Flat. Unlimited calls.*
- **Trial line:** *14-day free trial. Cancel anytime before day 14. From your dashboard, no phone call required.*

These four phrases should appear verbatim across the site. Don't rewrite them. If you need a variation for a smaller context, **truncate** (e.g. "Never miss a call.") — don't paraphrase.

## CTA verbs

**Primary CTA across every surface — marketing, product, email, SMS:**

- EN: **Get Capta →**
- ES: **Obtener Capta →**

**Never use:**

- ❌ Start Free Trial / Prueba Gratis
- ❌ Get Started
- ❌ Sign Up / Register
- ❌ Try It Free
- ❌ Learn More (as a primary)

**Secondary CTAs (contextual):**

- *Get a free call audit* → for cold prospect surfaces.
- *See pricing* → for mid-funnel.
- *Talk to Maria live* → for the demo widget.
- *Call us* → always paired with the phone number in mono tabular-nums.

## Capitalization

- **Sentence case** for headlines, subheadings, body. Not title case.
  - ✓ *Never miss a call again.*
  - ✗ Never Miss A Call Again.
- **ALL CAPS** only for kickers and meta labels (`PRICING`, `§ 01 · ABOUT`).
- **Proper nouns** follow their conventions: Capta, Maria, Twilio, Stripe, HVAC, EN/ES.
- **Trade names** lowercase except acronyms: "plumber," "electrician," "HVAC," "general contractor."
- **Product terms**: lowercase — "job card," "call log," "estimate," "invoice," "appointment." Not "Job Card."
- **Dashboard nav items**: sentence case: "Call logs," "Job cards," "Billing."

---

## Numbers + money

- **Always** render money, phone numbers, timestamps, and percentages in **JetBrains Mono with `tabular-nums`**.
- **Dollar amounts**: no space between `$` and number. Cents only when needed:
  - ✓ `$497` for flat pricing
  - ✓ `$497.00` in invoices where cents matter
  - ✓ `$4,764/year` for annual
- **Thousand separators** always: `$18,400`, `$4,764`, `10,000+`.
- **Percentages**: `62%` not `62 percent`.
- **Phone numbers**: `(830) 521-7133` (US format), rendered in Mono.
- **Times**: `2:15 pm` lowercase (not "2:15 PM" or "2:15PM").
- **Dates**: `Apr 22, 2026` for display; `2026-04-22` for metadata.

---

## Voice checks before shipping copy

Run every sentence through these filters:

1. **Would a master plumber say this?** If no, rewrite.
2. **Is there a number?** Specific numbers beat adjectives. "62% of calls go unanswered" beats "most calls go unanswered."
3. **Is there a promise?** Every section should make or keep a promise to the reader.
4. **Can I cut a word?** If yes, cut it.
5. **Is it an ad or a fact?** Prefer facts. The brand doesn't sell — it reports.

## Lexicon

### Use

*dispatch · estimate · job site · revenue · capture · crew · truck · off-hours · lead · ticket · route · shift · on-call · emergency · bilingual · fixed rate · flat · forward your number · SMS · text me · book the job · hang up · reach someone · in-bound*

### Don't use

- **SaaS / tech jargon**: omnichannel, synergize, AI LLM, agentic, workflow, orchestration, end-to-end
- **Startup-cute**: oopsie!, pro tip, life hack, game-changer, supercharge, disrupt, reimagine
- **Hollow modifiers**: seamless, robust, powerful, cutting-edge, next-gen, smart, intelligent, innovative, world-class
- **Corporate fluff**: stakeholder, enablement, solutions, leverage (as a verb), best-in-class
- **Adjective stacks**: "a smart, powerful, bilingual AI assistant" — pick one, earn it.

### On "AI"

Capta's product is AI-powered, but the brand doesn't lead with "AI." We lead with what Maria *does* — answers, books, follows up. When the prospect asks, we say **"AI receptionist"** — precise, honest, functional. We never say:

- ❌ Revolutionary AI
- ❌ AI-powered front office (corporate fluff)
- ❌ Our proprietary AI (who cares)

The product is named **Maria**. Not "our AI," not "the bot," not "the system." *Maria*.

## Tone calibration

### Warm (trust-building)

*"Morning. New job: drain clog at 4521 Oak Lane. Reply 1 to confirm."*

### Neutral (product explanation)

*"Capta answers in English or Spanish based on the caller's first word."*

### Firm (pricing, terms)

*"$497 a month. Flat. Cancel anytime before day 14."*

### Reassuring (objection handling)

*"Every call is transcribed. You see exactly what she said and what the caller said. If you spot a mistake, tell us once — she learns."*

### What we never do

- **Cute**: "She's always got your back." ❌
- **Hype**: "Capta is revolutionizing how contractors answer the phone!" ❌
- **Apologetic**: "We know you're busy, so we built..." ❌
- **Philosophical**: "At Capta, we believe every call deserves..." ❌

---

## Forbidden phrases

- "We're excited to announce"
- "Game-changer"
- "Revolutionary"
- "World-class"
- "At Capta, we believe..."
- "Passionate about..."
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Next-generation"
- "Unlock the power of..."
- "Democratize access to..."

If you catch one of these in a draft, cut it before shipping.

---

## The 5-second test

Before shipping any section of copy, read it aloud to yourself at a normal pace. If:

1. You **stumble** on a phrase → rewrite.
2. It sounds **promotional** → cut it down.
3. You can't **picture a specific buyer** reading it → rewrite.
4. You would feel **cringe** reading this at a job site → cut it.

The target isn't eloquent — it's **readable on a truck cab phone at 7 AM**.

## Bilingual discipline

- Spanish translations are **native**, not translated. Use Mexican Spanish by default (our Texas market).
- Verbs: use **tú** form for direct address, not **usted**. Home service is tú territory.
- Currency: still `$497` (same brand, same format).
- Phone: keep US format `(830) 521-7133` — don't reformat for ES.
- Dates: `22 de abril de 2026` in Spanish prose; `2026-04-22` for metadata.
